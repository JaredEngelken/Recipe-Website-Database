Thanksgiving Turkey

Amount:

  • 4+ Servings

Time:

  • 2.5+ Hours

Ingredients:

  • 1 - turkey
  • 2 - celery stalks
  • 2 - carrots
  • 1 - large onion
  • 1/2 cup - butter
  • 2 sprigs - fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs - fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs - fresh sage
  • 2 sprigs - fresh parsley
  • salt
  • fresh cracked pepper
  • 1 - turkey-sized oven bag
  • 1 tbs - flour

Instructions:

Select a Turkey:

A good rule of thumb is that a turkey weighs 1 lb. per serving without leftovers, and 1.5 lb. per serving with leftovers. For example, a 12 lb. bird would serve 12 people without extras. However, with leftovers a 12 lb. turkey would serve about 8 adults. If the calculated amount of turkey is more than 16 lbs., supplement the additional serving with turkey breasts. Too large of a turkey will not taste or cook as well.

Thaw the Turkey:

There are generally two methods of thawing: refridgerator and cold water, but refridgerator thawing is the safest in terms of bacteria. It will take 5 hours per pound of turkey to thaw in the refridgerator and 1 hour per pound in cold water.

Prepare the Turkey and Cook:

A turkey should always cook for a minumum of 2.5-3 hours plus 15 minutes for every extra 4 lbs.

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Slice up the celery and carrots and chop the onion into large wedges.
  3. Remove the neck and giblets from the body cavity of the turkey if they're in there.
  4. Rub the entire turkey in butter.
  5. Salt and pepper the bird liberally.
  6. Pack the body cavity with the vegetables and herbs.
  7. Sprinckle the flour into the bottom of the oven bag and lay extra vegetables on top, if they didn't all fit inside the turkey.
  8. Stuff the bird gently inside the oven bag breast-side down and seal the bag.
  9. Lay the turkey breast-side down in a large baking dish so that it appears to be upside-down.
  10. Cut several inch-long air vents in the top of the bag and inset an oven thermometer into the thickest part of the inner thigh, not touching bone.
  11. Insert the turkey into the oven and bake for the right amount of time or until the thermometer reads at least 165°F-180°F.
  12. When the turkey is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it rest in the bag for 15 minutes.
  13. Cut away the bag and serve!

    14. Optional:

    • Sprinkle the turkey in paprika for a strong browning effect.
    • Stuff the turkey with breadstuffing instead of the vegetables and herbs. Be sure to add an extra 30 minutes of cooking time.
    • Oil the turkey instead of rubbing it with butter.
    • Save the juices for gravy! Seesidesfor a good recipe.

Chicken Caprese

Amount:

  • 4 Servings

Time:

  • 35 Minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 - boneless chicken breasts
  • 1 tsp - dried oregano
  • 1 tsp - dried basil
  • 1/4 cup - olive oil or sun dried tomato oil
  • 1/4 cup - sun dried tomatoes
  • 2 tbs - brown sugar
  • 1 - large fresh mozzarella cheese ball
  • 2 - roma tomatoes
  • 1/3 cup - balsamic vinegar
  • 1 sprig - fresh rosemary
  • 12 - fresh basil leaves
  • 4 - garlic cloves
  • salt
  • fresh cracked pepper

Instructions:

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Cut a pocket about three-quarter of the way through on the thickest side of each breast, being careful not to cut all the way.
  3. Finely chop up the roseamry, mince the garlic, and thinly slice the tomatoes.
  4. Mix the rosemary with the oil.
  5. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, and dried herbs. Pour 1 teaspoon of oil over each breast, rubbing some of the seasoning inside the pockets.
  6. Fill each with 2 slices fresh tomato, 2 teaspoons sun dried tomato strips, one slice mozzarella cheese and basil leaves.
  7. Seal with 3-4 toothpicks diagonally to keep the filling inside while cooking.
  8. Heat 2 teaspoons of oil in a skillet or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and fry for 2 minutes on each side until golden.
  9. While the chicken is cooking, mix together the garlic, balsamic vinegar and brown sugar. Pour the mixture into the pan around the chicken; bring to a simmer while stirring occasionally, until the glaze has slightly thickened or about 2-3 minutes.
  10. Transfer the chicken to an oiled baking sheet and insert it into the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through so that a thermometer reads 165°F and the cheese has melted.
  11. Remove the chicken from the oven, take out the toothpicks, and drizzle the pan juices over top.
  12. Serve!

    13. Optional:

    • Top with chopped parsely
    • Pack sliced vegetables like zucchini, broccoli, asparagus, and cauliflower around the breasts while in the oven, topped with oil, salt, and pepper for a good side dish.
