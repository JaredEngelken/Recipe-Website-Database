Chocolate Souffle

Amount:

  • 2 Servings

Time:

  • 1/2-1 Hour

Ingredients:

  • 2 - 5-oz ramekins
  • Melted butter and sugar to prep ramekins
  • 1 tbs - butter
  • 1 tbs - flour
  • 1/4 cup - cold milk
  • 1 pinch - salt
  • 1 pinch - cayenne pepper
  • 2 oz - dark chocolate (~70% cocoa is good)
  • 1 - large egg yolk
  • 2 - large egg whites
  • 1 pinch - cream of tartar / a few drops of lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 tbs - white sugar

Instructions:

Prepare Ramekins:

  1. Apply melted butter all over the inside of the ramekin.
  2. Pour granulated sugar into ramekin and rotate around so that the sugar coats the entire inside.

Prepare Batter:

  1. Break up chocolate and put in metal bowl over a pot of hot water, just below simmering.
  2. Make a rue by melt butter in saucepan and then whisking in flour.
  3. Cook on medium heat for about a minute while stirring.
  4. Reduce heat to low and whisk in cold milk.
  5. Wait for the mixture to thicken then transfer the rue to the chocolate.
  6. Add the salt and cayenne pepper.
  7. Stir together with a spatula.
  8. Add an egg yolk and mix again. Then leave it over the hot water.

Prepare the Meringue:

  1. Place the egg whites in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add cream of tartar or lemon juice or vinegar.
  3. Whisk the egg whites till they're foamy.
  4. Add about a third of the sugar and continue whisking. After a bit, add another third and continue whisking. Again, after a bit, add the last third and continue whisking.
  5. Keep whisking until the egg whites resemble stiff shaving cream.

Bring it Together:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. Add a little less than half the egg whites to the chocolate mixture and stir it all together.
  3. Add the rest of the egg whites and carefully fold the mixture together until the egg whites are all gone and the mixture is a light brown. Do not over-stir!
  4. Transfer the better to the ramekins. Fill to the lip.
  5. Wipe away any drips so that the souffles rise straight.
  6. Place the ramekins on a pan and transfer them to the oven to bake at 375°F for 12-15 minutes or until they have risen above the edge of the ramekin.
  7. Remove from oven and serve immediately!

Optional:

  • Garnish the souffles with powdered cocoa, a mint sprig, or chocolate syrup.

Real Pumpkin Pie

Amount:

  • 2 Pies / 16 Servings

Time:

  • 3 Hours

Ingredients:

Crust Ingredients:

  • 2 cups - flour
  • 1 tsp - salt
  • 1/2 cup - shortening, lard, or fat
  • 4 tbs - butter
  • 2 tbs - cold water (add extra by the teaspoon as needed)

Filling Ingredients:

  • 1 Hokaido pumpkin or any small, sweet, fleshy pumpkin
  • 3 eggs
  • 3/4 cup - brown sugar
  • 2 tbs - molasses
  • 1 tsp - salt
  • 1/8 tsp - baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp - cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp - nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp - ginger
  • 1/4 tsp - cloves
  • 1 tsp - vanilla
  • 2 cups - scalded milk or 1 cup - cream and 1 cup - scalded milk
  • 2 - pie crusts

Instructions:

Crust Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Mix the ingredients together in a large bowl. Less mixing is better as too much makes the crust tough.
  3. Continue mixing and kneading until a semi-dry, semi-sticky dough ball forms.
  4. Separate in halves and press into pie pans.
  5. Bake at 425°F for about 10 minutes or until slightly browned. Remove the crusts from the oven if they begin to rise and then punch it back down.

Prepare Pumpkins:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Cut pumpkin into halves or quarters if it is large.
  3. Scoop out the seeds and stringy pulp.
  4. Set flesh side up on a cookie pan. It may be recommended to wrap the pumpkin pieces in foil.
  5. Bake for 1 hour at 350°F.

Make the Filling:

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F.
  2. Scoop the pulp from the skin and put in a food processor.
  3. Process the pulp until smooth.
  4. Spoon the pulp onto a towel.
  5. Wrap the towel up and press out the extra water so the pie isn't runny.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, mix together the eggs, sugar, molasses, vanilla, salt, baking soda, and spices.
  7. Stir in 2 cups of the pumpkin pulp.
  8. Add the milk.
  9. Stir thoroughly.

Bring it Together:

  1. Pour the filling into the pie shells.
  2. Bake for 10 minutes at 450°F.
  3. Reduce the heat to 350°F and bake for another 30-40 minutes.
  4. Check that the pies are done by sticking a toothpick into the center. If it comes out clean, then the pie is done baking.
  5. Wait for the pies to cool a bit and then serve!

Optional:

  • Garnish the pies with decorative dough pieces by rolling the dough flat and using cookie cutters or styling with a fork.
